## Formatting Best Practices

### Use of Tables
- **When to use**: Structured comparisons, financial data, contact lists
- **Keep concise**: 3-7 rows ideal, max 10 rows before splitting

### Use of Checkboxes
- **When to use**: Action items, deliverables tracking, success criteria monitoring
- **Format**: `- [ ] Action item ‚Äî Owner ‚Äî Due date`

### Use of Insight Markers
- ‚ö° **Surprises**: Information that contradicts expectations
- üí° **Client-originated**: Ideas client specifically requested
- üîÑ **Patterns**: Themes appearing 3+ times
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Contradictions**: Conflicting information needing resolution
- üîß **Consultant ideas**: Solutions we're proposing

### Evidence Citation Format
```markdown
**Quote:** "Exact words from stakeholder"  
**Source:** [Name], [unprompted/prompted], [Session date/document]  
**Confidence:** HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
```

### Quantification Examples
‚úÖ Good:
- "~$3.6M annual revenue from 3 streams"
- "15,000 visitors annually to 70,000 sq ft campus"
- "Controller spends 1 full day/month on reporting"

‚ùå Bad:
- "Significant revenue"
- "Many visitors"
- "Substantial time on reporting"

## Length Guidelines

**Total Document:**
- Target: 30-50 pages
- Minimum: 20 pages (for smaller engagements)
- Maximum: 60 pages (for complex multi-year programs)

**Part Breakdown:**
- Part 1 (Contract): 5-8 pages
- Part 2 (Discovery): 15-25 pages (the longest section)
- Part 3 (Execution): 8-12 pages
- Part 4 (Quick Reference): 3-5 pages

If any part exceeds these guidelines, consider:
- Breaking into sub-sections
- Moving detail to appendix
- Creating supplementary documents
