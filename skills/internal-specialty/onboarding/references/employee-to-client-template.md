# Employee-to-Client Onboarding Template

This template provides the operational onboarding structure for team members joining active client delivery teams. Use this structure for all employee onboarding to ensure consistency and operational readiness.

---

**Prepared by:** [Your Name], [Your Role]  
**Date:** [Current Date]  
**Contract Status:** [Signed Date] | [Active/In Progress/etc]

## Template Usage Guidelines

### Document Length Target
- **Minimum:** 15 pages (sufficient operational context)
- **Target:** 15-25 pages (comprehensive but scannable)
- **Maximum:** 25 pages (beyond this, move to strategic onboarding)

### Section Emphasis

**Most Important (Must Be Complete):**
- Contract Overview (what we're delivering)
- Key Stakeholders (who to work with and how)
- Immediate Action Items (what to do first)
- Meeting Cadence (when to show up)

**Important (Should Be Thorough):**
- Critical Pain Points (what we're solving)
- Technology Landscape (what systems exist)
- Strategic Priorities (what matters most)

**Supporting (Can Be Briefer):**
- Business Context (sufficient background)
- Quick Reference (helpful but not essential)

### Customization Notes

**Always Customize:**
- Welcome message to reflect client uniqueness
- Stakeholder interaction guidance (very client-specific)
- First week action items (role and timing specific)
- Cultural keywords (client's actual language)

**Usually Keep Standard:**
- Section structure (consistent across onboardings)
- Table formats (easier to scan when predictable)
- Checkbox format (standardize for tracking)

**Optional Sections:**
- Cultural Attributes (if especially relevant)
- Competitive Context (if sales-focused engagement)
- Client History (if long relationship with context needed)

### Quality Checklist Before Delivery

- [ ] All deliverable counts quantified (no "several" or "multiple")
- [ ] Every stakeholder has "Your Interaction" guidance
- [ ] 3-5 critical pain points identified with evidence
- [ ] Meeting schedule complete with times and attendees
- [ ] First week has 5-8 specific action items
- [ ] Quick Reference section has complete key contacts
- [ ] No TBD in critical sections (contract, stakeholders, meetings, actions)
- [ ] Document under 25 pages
- [ ] Scannable (tables, checkboxes, short paragraphs)
