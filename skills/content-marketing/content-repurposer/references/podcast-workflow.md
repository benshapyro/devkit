## Step 2: Transcription

### Get the Transcript

**Tools:**
- Descript (editing + transcription)
- Otter.ai (live + recorded)
- Rev.com (human transcription)
- Whisper (free, open-source)
- YouTube auto-captions (if video)

### Clean the Transcript

Remove:
- Filler words (um, uh, like, you know)
- False starts and corrections
- Repeated words/phrases
- Irrelevant tangents

Keep:
- Natural conversational flow
- Personality and voice
- Key quotes and insights
- Stories and examples

### Transcript Template

```markdown
# Transcript: [Episode Title]

**Episode:** [Number]
**Date:** [Recording date]
**Duration:** [XX:XX]
**Participants:** [Names]

---

[Speaker 1]: [Text...]

- [05:30] - [Topic 1]

- [28:00] - [Topic 3]
- [45:00] - Conclusion
```

## Step 5: Audiograms

### What is an Audiogram?

Audio clip + waveform visualization + text/captions = audiogram

Works well for:
- Audio-only podcasts on visual platforms
- Quote-focused content
- Teaser clips

### Tools

- Headliner (popular, easy)
- Wavve (simple, affordable)
- Descript (all-in-one editing)
- Canva (basic, free)

### Audiogram Workflow

1. **Select clip** (15-60 seconds with clear audio)
2. **Choose template** (platform-specific dimensions)
3. **Add waveform** (animated visualization)
4. **Add captions** (burn in or animate)
5. **Include branding** (logo, episode title, guest)
6. **Export** (MP4 format)
7. **Write caption** (hook + context + CTA)
8. **Schedule** across platforms

## Step 8: Video Content (if applicable)

### Full Episode on YouTube

If recording video:
- Upload full episode
- Add chapter markers from timestamps
- Create custom thumbnail
- Write SEO description
- Add cards and end screens

### YouTube Shorts/Reels/TikTok

From video recording, create:
- 3-5 vertical clips (60 sec max)
- Strong hook in first 3 seconds
- Captions burned in
- Native platform hashtags

### Clip Selection for Video

