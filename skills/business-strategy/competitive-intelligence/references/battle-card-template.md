# Battle Card Template

Complete template using the FIA (Fact-Impact-Act) framework for sales enablement.

## The FIA Framework

| Section | Purpose | Content Type |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Fact** | What's true about competitor | Company info, product details, positioning |
| **Impact** | Why this matters to sellers | When to use, competitive scenarios |
| **Act** | What to do about it | Talk tracks, objection handlers, proof points |

### Objection Handling

#### Objection: "[They're more established/mature]"

**Trap Question:**
"What specific capabilities are you evaluating? I want to make sure we're comparing apples to apples."

**Response:**
"[Competitor] has been in market longer, which means they also have more legacy architecture. Our platform was built [recently/cloud-native/etc.], which means [specific advantage]. For example, [customer X] chose us specifically because [reason]."

**Show:**
- [Demo section that proves the point]
- [Customer case study]

---

#### Objection: "[Their price is lower]"

**Trap Question:**
"What's included in that price? I want to make sure you're getting a complete comparison, including [implementation, support, add-ons]."

**Response:**
"When you look at total cost of ownership—including [implementation time, training, integrations, support tier]—our customers typically see [X% lower TCO / faster time-to-value]. [Customer X] initially thought [competitor] was cheaper, but after factoring in [specific costs], we were actually [comparable/better value]."

**Show:**
- TCO comparison spreadsheet
- Customer ROI story

---

#### Objection: "[They have feature X that you don't]"

**Trap Question:**
"Can you help me understand how you're planning to use [feature X]? I want to make sure we're solving the right problem."

**Response:**
"[Validate the need]. While we approach this differently with [our approach], the outcome for customers is [specific benefit]. In fact, [customer X] found that [our approach] actually [specific advantage]. [If on roadmap: This is also something we're actively developing—I can share our roadmap if helpful.]"

**Show:**
- [Alternative solution demo]
- [Customer story about solving same problem differently]

---

### Talk Tracks

#### Discovery Phase

**Opening:**
"I understand you're also looking at [competitor]. They're a solid option for [use case]. Most teams we talk to are evaluating them alongside us because [shared value prop]. Where we see teams ultimately choose us is when [key differentiator scenario]. Is that something you're prioritizing?"

**Probing Questions:**
- "What's driving your evaluation of [competitor] specifically?"
- "What would it take for them to win your business?"
- "What concerns, if any, do you have about [competitor]?"

#### Demo/Evaluation Phase

**Competitive Positioning:**
"Let me show you how we approach [capability] differently than [competitor]. While they [their approach], we [our approach]. This matters because [customer benefit]. [Demo the specific capability]."

**Setting Traps:**
"One thing I'd encourage you to ask [competitor] is [question that exposes their weakness]. For some customers that's not a big deal, but for teams like yours who [need], it can become [problem]."

#### Closing Phase

**Competitive Summary:**
"Based on what you've shared about [their priorities], here's why teams in your situation typically choose us over [competitor]:

1. [Differentiator 1 tied to their stated need]
2. [Differentiator 2 tied to their stated need]
3. [Differentiator 3 tied to their stated need]

What questions do you have about any of those?"

## Battle Card Types

### 1. Quick Dismiss Card
For early-stage objection handling.

```markdown
# [Competitor] - Quick Dismiss

**When Prospect Mentions Them:**
"[Competitor] is a good option for [their sweet spot]. Where we see customers choose us instead is when [our differentiation]. What's driving your evaluation?"

**Key Differentiators (Top 3):**
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]

**If They Push Back:**
→ Use full battle card
```

### 2. Feature Battle Card
For deep technical comparisons.

```markdown
# [Competitor] - Feature Comparison

| Feature | Us | Them | Notes |
|---------|------|------|-------|
| [Feature 1] | ✓✓ | ✓ | [Detail] |
...
```

### 3. Pricing Battle Card
For pricing-focused objections.

```markdown
# [Competitor] - Pricing Response

**Their Pricing Model:** [Description]
**Our Pricing Model:** [Description]

**Why We're Better Value:**
1. [TCO factor 1]
2. [TCO factor 2]

**Common Pricing Traps:**
- [Hidden cost they have]
- [Add-on they charge for]
```

## Best Practices

### Do
- Update at least quarterly
- Use customer-ready language (what reps can say word-for-word)
- Include specific proof points
- Be honest about competitor strengths
- Test with sales team before publishing

### Don't
- Include company history/founding story (not actionable)
- Use neutral language (give content positive/negative charge)
- Skip the "When We Lose" section
- Publish without sales input
- Let cards go stale
