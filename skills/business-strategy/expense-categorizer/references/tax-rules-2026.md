## Meals & Entertainment Deductions

### Meal Deductions (50%)

**Fully Deductible at 50%:**
- Business meals with clients, customers, or prospects
- Meals during business travel (away from tax home)
- Team meals with substantial business discussion
- Meals at business conventions or conferences

**Requirements for Deduction:**
1. **Ordinary and Necessary:** Common in your trade/business
2. **Not Lavish:** Reasonable under circumstances
3. **Business Present:** Taxpayer or employee present
4. **Business Purpose:** Directly related to business

**Documentation Required:**
- Amount (itemized receipt for $75+)
- Date and place
- Business purpose
- Business relationship of attendees
- Names of attendees

### Entertainment Deductions (0%)

**NOT Deductible (since TCJA 2017):**
- Tickets to sporting events, concerts, theater
- Golf outings, country club dues
- Skyboxes and luxury suites
- Recreational facilities
- Social entertainment

**Exception:** Entertainment may be deductible if:
- Treated as compensation to employee (and reported as income)
- Made available to general public
- Sold to customers

### Meal vs. Entertainment

| Scenario | Deductible | Rate | Notes |
|----------|------------|------|-------|
| Dinner with client | Yes | 50% | Business discussion |
| Lunch during meeting | Yes | 50% | Business purpose |
| Client golf + lunch | Lunch only | 50% | Golf is entertainment |
| Team happy hour | Usually | 50% | Must have business purpose |
| Holiday party | Yes | 50% | Annual employee event |
| Concert tickets | No | 0% | Entertainment |
| Skybox at game | No | 0% | Entertainment |
| Food at skybox | Maybe | 50% | If separately stated |

## Travel Deductions

### Fully Deductible (100%)

- Airfare (coach/economy standard)
- Lodging (reasonable for location)
- Ground transportation (taxi, Uber, rental car)
- Baggage fees
- Tips for service providers
- Laundry/dry cleaning (extended trips)
- Business phone calls
- Internet fees
- Conference registration

### Partially Deductible (50%)

- Meals during travel (away from tax home)

### Tax Home Rules

**Away from Tax Home:** Must be away overnight or long enough to require sleep/rest

**Tax Home Definition:**
- Your regular place of business (not where you live)
- If no regular place of business, your regular abode
- If neither, wherever you work

**Same-Day Travel:** Generally NOT deductible for meals (not away from tax home)

### Travel Documentation

For all travel expenses:
- Date of travel
- Destination
- Business purpose
- Receipts for expenses $75+
- Lodging receipts (any amount)

## Equipment & Assets

### De Minimis Safe Harbor ($2,500)

**Rule:** Items costing $2,500 or less per invoice (or per item for bulk purchases) can be expensed immediately rather than capitalized.

**Requirements:**
- Written accounting policy in place at start of year
- Treat consistently for financial accounting
- Apply to all eligible items

**Examples:**
- Computer: $2,400 → Expense immediately
- Computer: $3,000 → Must capitalize and depreciate
- 10 monitors @ $300 each → Each can be expensed

### Section 179 Deduction

**2026 Limits (estimated):**
- Maximum Deduction: ~$1,220,000
- Phase-out Threshold: ~$3,050,000

**Eligible Property:**
- Tangible personal property (equipment, machinery)
- Computers and software
- Certain qualified improvement property
- Vehicles (with limitations)

### Bonus Depreciation

**2026 Rate:** 60% (phasing down from 100% in 2022)

**Applies To:**
- New and used property
- Most tangible property with recovery period ≤20 years
- Qualified improvement property

### Depreciation Schedules

| Asset Type | Recovery Period |
|------------|-----------------|
| Computers & Office Equipment | 5 years |
| Office Furniture | 7 years |
| Vehicles | 5 years |
| Buildings (non-residential) | 39 years |
| Buildings (residential) | 27.5 years |
| Land Improvements | 15 years |

## Vehicle Expenses

### Standard Mileage Rate Method

**2026 Rate:** 67 cents per mile

**When to Use:**
- Simple record keeping
- Lower actual costs
- Personal vehicle used for business

**Requirements:**
- Mileage log with:
  - Date
  - Miles driven
  - Business purpose
  - Starting and ending locations

**What's Included:**
- Gas and oil
- Insurance
- Repairs and maintenance
- Depreciation
- Registration

### Actual Expense Method

**Calculate:**
- Total vehicle expenses for year
- Multiply by business use percentage

**Expenses Included:**
- Gas and oil
- Insurance
- Repairs and maintenance
- Lease payments
- Depreciation (if owned)
- Interest on car loan
- Garage rent
- Parking and tolls (always deductible separately)

**Requirements:**
- Detailed records of all expenses
- Mileage log for business/personal allocation

### Commuting

**NOT Deductible:**
- Home to regular workplace
- Regular workplace to home

**Deductible:**
- Workplace to workplace (during day)
- Home to temporary workplace (if home is your tax home)
- Travel between clients/job sites

## Home Office Deduction

### Requirements

Must be used:
1. **Regularly and Exclusively** for business
2. **Principal Place of Business** OR
3. **Meet Clients/Customers** OR
4. **Separate Structure** used for business

### Simplified Method

**Rate:** $5 per square foot (max 300 sq ft = $1,500)

**Benefits:**
- No depreciation calculations
- No recapture on sale
- Simple record keeping

### Regular Method

**Calculate:**
- Business use percentage (square footage)
- Apply to home expenses:
  - Mortgage interest/rent
  - Utilities
  - Insurance
  - Repairs
  - Depreciation

## Record Keeping Requirements

### General Rules

| Expense Type | Record Keeping |
|--------------|---------------|
| Travel | Diary/log + receipts |
| Meals | Documentation + receipts ($75+) |
| Entertainment | N/A (not deductible) |
| Vehicle | Mileage log |
| Equipment | Purchase records |

### Receipt Requirements

**IRS Receipt Requirements:**
- Required for expenses $75 and over
- Required for ALL lodging (any amount)
- Should show:
  - Amount
  - Date
  - Place
  - Business purpose

**Exception:** If receipt is lost, can use other evidence (bank statement, etc.) but less reliable

### Retention Period

Keep records for:
- **3 years** from date of filing (standard audit period)
- **6 years** if significant underreporting possible
- **7 years** if deducting bad debts or worthless securities
- **Indefinitely** for property purchase records (capital gains)

## Common Audit Triggers

### High-Risk Expense Categories

1. **Meals & Entertainment:** Most frequently audited
2. **Travel:** Especially mixed business/personal trips
3. **Vehicle:** Business use percentage claims
4. **Home Office:** Exclusive use requirement
5. **Miscellaneous:** Vague or large amounts

### Red Flags

- Round numbers (suggests estimates)
- Unusually high deductions for industry
- Inconsistent patterns year-to-year
- Missing documentation
- Personal expenses mixed with business
- Excessive meals as percentage of revenue

### Best Practices

1. **Document business purpose** for every expense
2. **Keep receipts** even under $75
3. **Separate business and personal** clearly
4. **Use dedicated business accounts**
5. **Review expenses before filing**
6. **Consult tax professional** for complex situations
